How to play:

You have two minutes to create poker hands. The countdown timer is located on the top left side of the game.

Poker hands are created by pressing your finger on a card and dragging up, down, left or right. Diagonal selection is only allowed on the first level. As you drag the cards will dim indicating that they have been selected. Releasing your finger causes the game to evaluate the hand. Be sure to release while selecting your last card or the selection will not be made.

If no valid selection was made, the cards will remain. If a valid hand is made the cards are discarded and points are given. The remaining cards fall down to fill the empty positions.

Valid hands include all normal poker hands added with three and four card versions of a flush or straight.

Straight examples (special wheel straights):

  • A, 2, 3
  • A, 2, 3, 4
  • A, 2, 3, 4, 5

Poker hands are assigned points based on the complexity of the hand (pair, three of a kind, etc.) in addition to an assigned value of the individual cards. Points are listed on the top right side of the game.

Occasionally you may run out of valid poker hands to select. A spin button is used to spin each column of cards similar to a slot machine. There is a 200 point penalty each time the spin button is used.
